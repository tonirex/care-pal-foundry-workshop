# Heart Failure — Medication

> verified export before go-live. Not medical advice.

Heart failure medicines help your heart work better, control blood pressure, and remove extra fluid.
Take them exactly as prescribed, even when you feel well.

## Common medicine types
- Water pills (diuretics) reduce fluid — you may pass urine more often.
- ACE inhibitors / ARBs and beta-blockers ease the heart's workload.
- Take doses at the same times daily; use a pillbox or phone reminder.

## Staying safe
- Do not stop or change a dose on your own. If you miss one, do not double up.
- Tell your team about side effects like dizziness, cough, or swelling.
- Check before using painkillers (e.g. NSAIDs) or herbal supplements — some worsen heart failure.

## When to seek help
For severe breathlessness, fainting, or chest pain, call 995 or go to A&E. For ongoing side effects
or questions about timing, contact your care team for a timely review.
