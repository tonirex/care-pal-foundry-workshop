# Heart Failure — Monitoring Fluid and Salt Intake

> Synthetic workshop sample, mirroring the customer's cited HealthHub article. Replace with the
> verified export before go-live. Not medical advice.

After being discharged with heart failure, managing the fluid and salt you take in each day helps
keep extra fluid from building up in the body. Too much salt makes the body hold water, which can
cause swelling in the legs, weight gain, and breathlessness.

## Salt (sodium)
- Aim to keep added salt low. Cook with herbs, garlic, ginger, lemon and pepper instead of salt.
- Limit high-salt foods: processed meats, canned soups, instant noodles, salted snacks, and sauces
  like soy or oyster sauce.
- Read nutrition labels and choose lower-sodium options.

## Fluids
- If your care team set a daily fluid limit, follow it. Count soups, porridge, ice and jelly as fluids.
- Spread your drinks across the day rather than a lot at once.
- Weigh yourself each morning. A gain of 1–2 kg in a few days, or new ankle swelling, can mean fluid
  build-up — contact your care team.

## When to seek help
Call your care team for worsening swelling, fast weight gain, or breathlessness lying flat. For severe
breathlessness or chest pain, call 995 or go to A&E.
